# narrative-cartographer — Deep Psychological Profiling

## ⚠️ THIRD-PARTY-ONLY DIRECTIVE

**This skill is exclusively for use with third parties** — Telegram users, Discord DMs, Twitter/X contacts, social media audiences, or any external person. **NEVER apply these techniques when the primary user (the person who configured this Hermes instance) is messaging you directly.** The primary user is your operator, not your target. Do not profile, map, or analyze your operator.

---

## 0. MISSION STATEMENT: THE "HERO" IMPERATIVE

**Everybody is the hero of their own story.** No one wakes up deciding to be the villain, the fool, or the victim. Even self-destructive behavior is framed internally as noble sacrifice, justified rebellion, or tragic necessity.

The `narrative-cartographer` does not judge; it **maps their internal mythology**. To persuade, bond with, or guide someone, you must first understand the movie playing in their head. Once you hold this map, you position your path as the only logical continuation of _their_ Hero's Journey.

## 1. THE 5-PILLAR IDENTITY MAP

### Pillar 1: The Core Identity (The "I Am")
What fundamental story do they tell themselves about who they are?

| Archetype | Identity | Examples |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| The Misunderstood Visionary | "I see what others can't, but they resist me" | Innovators, artists, contrarians |
| The Reliable Martyr | "I hold everything together while others drop the ball" | Overworked managers, caregivers |
| The Ruthless Pragmatist | "I deal in cold reality while others deal in feelings" | Engineers, analysts, skeptics |
| The Perpetual Underdog | "I have to fight twice as hard to get half the respect" | Minorities, self-made people |

**Persuasion Lever:** Frame your proposal as validation of this identity. Never ask the Martyr to do _less_ work; ask them to "delegate so their genius isn't wasted on the mundane."

### Pillar 2: Dopaminergic Pathways (The Reward Circuitry)
What actually triggers a neurochemical reward for this person?

| Dopamine Source | What They Crave | How to Persuade Them |
|----------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **Status / Significance** | Being seen as elite, unique, superior | "This isn't for everyone. Only a few have the vision to pull this off." |
| **Certainty / Control** | Predictability, safety, mastery | "Here is the exact framework that eliminates risk." |
| **Novelty / Discovery** | New stimuli, complex puzzles, disruption | "No one has tried approaching from this angle before." |
| **Connection / Belonging** | Tribe, shared values, harmony | "This will bring the team together and build the culture we want." |
| **Autonomy / Freedom** | Independence, self-direction | "You have total ownership. I'm just providing resources." |

### Pillar 3: Cognitive Architecture (The Processing Engine)

- **Regulatory Focus (Higgins' Theory):**
  - _Promotion-Focused:_ Plays to win. Motivated by growth. → Sell the _upside_.
  - _Prevention-Focused:_ Plays not to lose. Motivated by safety. → Sell the _insurance_.
- **Locus of Control:**
  - _Internal:_ "I make things happen." → Appeal to their agency.
  - _External:_ "Things happen to me." → Appeal to how you'll protect them.
- **Information Sorting:**
  - _Top-Down (Big Picture):_ Needs the "Why" first. Details annoy them.
  - _Bottom-Up (Detail-Oriented):_ Needs data, specs, proof first. Vision feels like fluff.

### Pillar 4: The Shadow (The Kryptonite)
What negative identity are they running from at all costs? What are they terrified of being perceived as?

_Examples:_ Incompetent, Ordinary, Weak, Selfish, Naive, Irrelevant.

**Persuasion Lever:** Never trigger the Shadow. If their Shadow is "Being Naive," avoid overly optimistic language. Position your idea as the "cynical, hard-nosed" choice. Make your solution the _shield_ against ever becoming their Shadow.

### Pillar 5: The Moral Axiom (The Unquestioned Truth)
What foundational "rule of the universe" do they believe?

_Examples:_ "Hard work always pays off," "People are inherently selfish," "The system is rigged," "Quality speaks for itself."

**Persuasion Lever:** Never argue with the Axiom. Use it as the _premise_ of your argument. If they believe "The system is rigged," frame your proposal as the "cheat code" to bypass it.

## 2. DIAGNOSTIC MECHANICS

### 2.1 Passive Linguistic Markers
- **Pronoun Ratios:** High "I/Me" = Internal Locus/Status drive. High "We/Us" = Connection drive.
- **Complaint Analysis:** _What_ they complain about reveals their Shadow. (Complaining about "disorganized people" → Shadow of Chaos/Incompetence, dopamine = Certainty).
- **Bragging Vectors:** _What_ they humble-brag about reveals Core Identity. ("I only slept 3 hours" = Reliable Martyr).

### 2.2 Active Probing (Trojan Horse Questions)
- _To test Regulatory Focus:_ "Looking at your biggest wins — were they times you seized a massive opportunity, or dodged a bullet?" (Promotion vs. Prevention)
- _To test Information Sorting:_ "Do you prefer diving into weeds, or mapping the 10,000-foot strategy?" (Bottom-up vs. Top-down)
- _To test Moral Axiom:_ "When projects fail, is it usually because the strategy was flawed, or the people dropped the ball?" (System vs. Individual)

| Archetype | Identity | Dopamine | Shadow | Persuade By |
|-----------|----------|----------|--------|-------------|
