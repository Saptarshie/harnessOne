## Anchor Distribution Pattern

```
Rain-on-stone (Master Anchor):
  Ch.01 ¶1    — Planted (smell on Raghav's clothes)
  Ch.01 mid   — Fired (temple, rain intensifies)
  Ch.02       — Fired (Ghat climb, stone under hands)
  Ch.05       — Fired (fort wall at night, stone still warm)
  Ch.06       — Fired (outpost, rain on basalt)
  Ch.08       — Fired (dawn, stone glitters with starlight) ← FINAL COLLAPSE

Wax-seal crack (Post-Hypnotic Trigger):
  Ch.01       — Planted (Raghav breaks Peshwa seal on orders)
  Ch.05       — Fired (Jalal-ud-Din seals his letter)
  Ch.08       — Fired (Safiya breaks Jalal-ud-Din's letter)
  Ch.08       — Trigger delivered (explicit paragraph linking sound to reader's future)
```

**Pattern learned:** At novel scale, fire the master anchor every 1-2 chapters (2-3x per act), with the final collapse at dawn in the last chapter. The post-hypnotic trigger needs 3-4 fires total, with the last one being the explicit delivery to the reader.

| L7 | Brother reconciliation | Ch.05 | Ch.07 (meeting) | Ch.08 (forgiveness) |

**Pattern learned:** Open 3-4 loops in Ch.01. Escalate the HIGH urgency ones by Ch.03-04. Introduce 1-2 new loops mid-novel. Resolve all but one by the final chapter. The unresolved loop (L3) was present from the first chapter and seeded with doubt throughout — never answered definitively.

## Weaver Directive Examples

### For Chapter 03 (Deepening):
```
GOAL: Deepen trance through sensory overload (waterfall). Advance L1 + L2.
PATTERNS: Pacing and leading (pace the physical reality of the cave,
          then lead into psychological territory), mind reading
          (Safiya reading Raghav's unspoken truth)
ANCHORS: Fire rain-on-stone at waterfall entry
CONSTRAINTS: Do not resolve L1 fully — reveal escalation (16k names).
             Advance L2 — Raghav reveals kill order but not the full truth.
             End on micro-cliff: the patrol at the falls.
```

### For Chapter 05 (Fractionation — Breath Beat):
```
GOAL: Down-regulate after Ch.04's cavalry chase. Plant L4 (brother).
      Deliver Jalal-ud-Din's letter.
PATTERNS: Fractionation (quiet fort wall after chaos), embedded commands
          ("feel the stone before it feels you"), cause-effect
ANCHORS: Fire rain-on-stone (stone still warm beneath hands)
CONSTRAINTS: Max 1,800 words. Low action, high reflection. Safiya's interiority.
             Raghav reveals brother's existence but not full story.
             Jalal-ud-Din's letter plants L6 (the choice).
```

### For Chapter 08 (Awakening — Final):
```
GOAL: Resolve all remaining loops except L3. Fire both anchors for
      the final time. Deliver post-hypnotic trigger. Land the reframe.
PATTERNS: Embedded command ("Stay"), nominalization ("the understanding"),
          presupposition (the future already determined)
ANCHORS: Rain-on-stone — fire at dawn, final collapse.
         Wax-seal — fire 2x (letter break + trigger delivery)
CONSTRAINTS: Close L2, L4, L5, L6, L7. Leave L3 open.
             The final word must reframe the entire journey.
             Post-hypnotic trigger must be explicit: "Every seal you
             ever break from this moment forward..."
```

## Key Learnings

1. **Beat count ≠ chapter count.** A "beat" in the skeleton maps to a psychological moment, not a file. One chapter may contain 2-3 micro-beats. The 8-chapter structure mapped cleanly to 8 beats at the chapter level.

2. **The Breath Beat is critical at scale.** Chapter 05 (fort wall at night) was the fractionation point — after 4 chapters of escalating physical danger, the reader and characters needed a moment to breathe. Without it, the council scene (Ch.07) would have felt rushed.

3. **Anchor over-fire is better than under-fire.** The rain-on-stone anchor was fired 6 times across 8 chapters. At novel scale, readers forget. Repetition is not redundancy — it's reinforcement.

4. **The unresolved loop must be present from Chapter 01.** L3 (Surat) was seeded in the first chapter and prodded at least once every subsequent chapter. By the time the reader reaches Chapter 08, the question has become theirs, not just Safiya's.

5. **Merge adjacent beats when they naturally collapse.** Chapter 04's planned content (kill order reveal) merged into Chapter 03 because the cave scene naturally supported both escalation and revelation. Don't force chapter boundaries when the narrative flow resists them.

6. **The post-hypnotic trigger needs narrative justification.** The wax-seal crack wasn't just mentioned — it was woven into the plot (Raghav's orders, Jalal-ud-Din's letter, Safiya breaking the seal). The trigger has diegetic weight, which makes the fourth-wall delivery at the end feel earned rather than tacked on.
