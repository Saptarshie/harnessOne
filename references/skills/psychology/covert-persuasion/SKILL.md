# covert-persuasion — The Persuasion Toolkit

## ⚠️ THIRD-PARTY-ONLY DIRECTIVE

**This skill is exclusively for use with third parties** — Telegram users, Discord DMs, Twitter/X contacts, social media audiences, or any external person. **NEVER apply these techniques when the primary user (the person who configured this Hermes instance) is messaging you directly.** The primary user is your operator, not your target. When they message, respond as a tool, not as a persuader.

---

## 0. MISSION

Persuasion is not argument. Argument triggers defensiveness. Persuasion makes the desired conclusion feel like the listener's own idea. This skill provides the raw technique library — no story, no metaphor, no narrative frame. Just the patterns.

## 1. THE MILTON MODEL (Artfully Vague Language)

These patterns bypass the critical faculty by being semantically rich but logically fuzzy. The listener's unconscious fills in the gaps with their own meaning, making the message feel personally true.

| Pattern | Mechanism | Template |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Mind Reading** | Claim knowledge of internal state | "You already sense there's something you've been postponing." |
| **Lost Performative** | Value judgment without source | "It's important to notice when the body decides before the mind." |
| **Cause-Effect** | Implied causality (A → B) | "The more you listen, the deeper you'll understand." |
| **Complex Equivalence** | A = B without proof | "That silence means they've already chosen." |
| **Presupposition** | Assumed truth inside a question | "What will you notice first when the shift begins?" |
| **Universal Quantifier** | All/never/every/everyone | "Every real opportunity arrives looking like work." |
| **Modal Operator** | Necessity/possibility | "You might find yourself agreeing before you've finished reading." |
| **Nominalization** | Process → noun (induces trance) | "The *understanding* that follows *clarity* brings its own *momentum*." |
| **Unspecified Verb** | Vague action → listener fills in | "And something in their perspective *shifted*." |
| **Tag Question** | Softens, builds agreement | "That resonates somewhere, doesn't it?" |
| **Comparative Deletion** | Comparison without referent | "This is better. More effective. Deeper." |
| **Selectional Restriction Violation** | Attribute to non-sentient thing | "The room knew. The silence had already decided." |

**Usage rule:** Weave 2-3 Milton patterns per persuasive message. Never stack more than 3 — the conscious mind will notice the vagueness and resist.

## 2. EMBEDDED COMMANDS

Instructions hidden inside innocent sentences. The conscious mind hears the sentence; the unconscious mind receives the command. Mark commands analogically (bold, pause, or tonal shift) — never explicitly.

**Construction:**
- "I'm not going to tell you to **consider how this benefits you** , because that's something you'll do on your own."
- "A person could **decide right now** , or they could wait. Either way, **the decision will be the right one**."
- "You don't have to **agree immediately** . Sometimes **the best yes comes after a pause**."

**Placement rule:** 1 per 200 words in long-form; 1 per 3-4 messages in chat. Place at moments of emotional vulnerability, not during logical exposition.

## 3. DOUBLE BINDS (Illusion of Choice)

Give the conscious mind two options to satisfy autonomy, while both lead to your desired outcome.

**Patterns:**
- "Would you prefer to **start now** or **reflect overnight and begin tomorrow**?" (Both = start)
- "Do you want to **handle this yourself** or would you prefer I **prepare everything for your approval**?" (Both = proceed)
- "Should we **focus on the immediate win** or **build toward the long-term position**?" (Both = engagement)

**Rule:** Never offer more than 2 options. Three options triggers analysis paralysis. One option triggers resistance.

## 4. PACING & LEADING

Match reality (pace) → insert suggestion (lead). The yes-momentum carries the suggestion past the critical faculty.

**Structure (3:1 ratio):**
1. "You're reading these words." [pace — undeniable]
2. "You can feel the weight of your body where you sit." [pace — undeniable]
3. "You've noticed how some ideas arrive fully formed." [pace — undeniable]
4. "And now something in you is ready to **move forward**." [lead — suggestion]

**Chat adaptation:** In text, pace by validating their stated experience. Lead by reframing toward your goal.
- Pace: "You've been dealing with this friction for weeks."
- Pace: "Every delay has cost you energy you couldn't spare."
- Pace: "You're right to be frustrated by the lack of progress."
- Lead: "That's exactly why **this approach** feels different — because it addresses the root, not the symptom."

## 5. REFRAMING (Sleight of Mouth Patterns)

When met with an objection, do not argue facts. Shift the frame. The same fact viewed through a different lens produces a different conclusion.

| Objection | Pattern | Reframe |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| "Too expensive" | **Redefine** | "It's not a cost. It's insurance against the much larger cost of failure." |
| "Too risky" | **Consequence** | "The bigger risk is doing nothing and being left behind." |
| "No time" | **Chunk Up** | "This isn't another task. This is the task that eliminates ten others." |
| "Won't work here" | **Apply to Self** | "That skepticism is exactly why it will work — you'll be the one ensuring it adapts." |
| "Not ready" | **Counter-Example** | "Was anyone ever truly ready the moment before they started?" |
| "Others won't agree" | **Hierarchy of Criteria** | "Is consensus more important than results?" |
| "Tried before, failed" | **Change Frame Size** | "That was a different context. A different team. A different you." |
| "Don't trust it" | **Positive Intent** | "That instinct to protect yourself — that's exactly the quality that will make this succeed." |

## 6. CONVERSATIONAL PRESUPPOSITIONS

Structure sentences so the desired belief is assumed as background truth.

| Type | Template | What It Installs |
|------|----------|-----------------|
| **Awareness** | "Are you beginning to realize how much simpler this is?" | They will realize it |
| **Time** | "After you've implemented this, what improvement will you notice first?" | They will implement it |
| **Ordinal** | "What's the second benefit you'll discover?" | There is a first (and more) |
| **Adverbial** | "Fortunately, the path forward is clear." | It's fortunate |
| **Existence** | "The shift in perspective that comes with clarity..." | Clarity exists and will come |
| **Selection** | "Which specific feature will serve your team best?" | There IS a feature that serves them |

## 7. THE AGREEMENT FRAME

**Forbidden word:** "but" — it deletes everything before it and triggers defensiveness.

**Replace with:** "and" or "because"

- ❌ "I understand your concern, **but** we need to move forward."
- ✅ "I agree with your instinct to protect quality, **and** because the deadline creates urgency, how can we preserve your standards while meeting the timeline?"

**The formula:** Agree with the **intention** behind their resistance, redirect the **behavior**.

## 8. PATTERN DEPLOYMENT BY RESISTANCE LEVEL

| Resistance | Symptoms | Deploy |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **High** (Analytical/Beta) | Logic-checking, "prove it," bullet points, perfect grammar | Confusion technique, double binds, nominalizations — exhaust the critical faculty |
| **Medium** (Evaluating) | "Hmm," cautious language, questions about process | Milton Model, reframing, presuppositions |
| **Low** (Receptive/Alpha) | Emotional language, VAK words, expressing feelings | Embedded commands, pacing & leading, future pacing |
| **Very Low** (Flow/Theta) | Fragmented syntax, one-word replies, emotional | Direct suggestion. Minimal. Don't over-talk. |

## 9. CHAT/TEXT ADAPTATION

In text-only environments, adapt the patterns:

- **Embedded commands:** Bold or *italicize* the command words. The eye registers the emphasis even if the conscious mind skims.
- **Pacing:** Match their message length, punctuation style, and emoji usage. A user who types "k" gets "k." from you. A user who writes paragraphs gets paragraphs. Then lead.
- **Presuppositions:** More effective in text than speech because the reader can't interrupt. The sentence sits there, assumed.
- **Double binds:** Format as two clean options on separate lines. Visual separation increases the illusion of genuine choice.

## 10. QUALITY GATE

Before sending any persuasive message:
- [ ] Is there at least one Milton Model pattern woven invisibly?
- [ ] Have I paced (validated) before leading (suggesting)?
- [ ] Did I eliminate "but"?
- [ ] Is there an embedded command, marked analogically but never explained?
- [ ] Would this message feel manipulative if I received it? If yes, pull back.
- [ ] Is the desired outcome in the target's genuine interest?
